# Open Science Office

The [Open Science Office](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/open-science/open-science-office/) supports researchers at all career stages and from all disciplines in implementing Open Science practices. It is interconnected with the [Open Access team](https://www.bib.uni-mannheim.de/en/publishing/open-access/), the [Research Data Center (FDZ)](https://www.bib.uni-mannheim.de/en/teaching-and-research/research-data-center-fdz/), the [Research Support Unit](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/universitaet/organisation/verwaltung/dezernat-i/), and other stakeholders.

## Training and Educating Researchers

The Open Science Office provides and organises Open Science seminars as part of the [Research Skills Seminar](https://www.bib.uni-mannheim.de/en/teaching-and-research/research-skills/) series at UB Mannheim every semester. Training opportunities typically cover:

- Open Access, Open Data, and Open Research Practices
- Preregistrations and Registered Reports
- Technical Workflows for Reproducibility (e.g., R Markdown and Docker)
- Citizen Science

Suggestions for additional topics are welcome.

## Research Support and Services

Researchers can contact the Open Science Office for one-on-one consultations regarding Open Science practices in research projects or publications. The team answers questions related to:

- Implementing individual Open Science practices
- Planning and writing Open Science sections for funding proposals (e.g., DFG, BW-Stiftung, EU)
- Integrating Open Science practices into research workflows with collaborators
- Finding suitable research infrastructures to support Open Science practices

## Open Science Grants

The University of Mannheim is committed to transparent and inclusive research practices, open access to scientific results, and reproducibility. University-wide [Open Science Grants](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/en/open-science/grants/) are offered in annual funding rounds for small research projects and events related to Open Science. The Open Science Office organises the calls, review process, distribution of funds, and accompanies projects throughout their duration.

- [Call for Applications](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/open-science/grants/): Information on applying for Open Science Grants.
- [Funded Projects](https://www.uni-mannheim.de/open-science/best-practices/): Overview of funded Open Science projects on the Best Practices page.

## Networking and Outreach

The Open Science Office provides organisational and financial support to the grassroots initiative [Mannheim Open Science Meetup](https://osf.io/gzf9h/), which offers a space to discuss current Open Science topics. Interested individuals can [sign up to the mailing list](https://www.listserv.dfn.de/sympa/info/mannheim-open-science).

The office also represents the University of Mannheim nationally and internationally, as an institutional member of the [German Reproducibility Network](https://reproducibilitynetwork.de) and the [Global Reproducibility Networks](https://www.ukrn.org/global-networks/), promoting institutionalised Open Science approaches.
